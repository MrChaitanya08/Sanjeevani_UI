What is cancer?

A disease caused by an uninhibited growth of abnormal cells in any part of the body.

How can cancer be cured?

There are three ways of curing, restricting and managing cancerous cells.

Medical Oncology:

Cancer is treated through chemotherapy. Chemotherapy is a drug treatment that uses powerful chemicals to kill the fast growing cancerous cells. It is the most commonly used way of treatment.

Radiation Oncology:

Radiation therapy is the use of precisely calculated doses of high energy X-rays to the affected areas. It is a pain free treatment.

Surgical Oncology:

Surgical management of cancer has evolved in many ways. While surgery alone is enough for early cancers, most advanced cancers require a multimodality treatment which includes managing the cancer with surgery, radiotherapy and chemotherapy.
